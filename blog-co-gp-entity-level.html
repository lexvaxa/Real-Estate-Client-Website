Building a Diversified Investment Portfolio

By Jonathan Gerber Published Dec 5, 2026 5 Min Read

Strategic platform capitalization and co-GP placements are transforming how family offices and managers diversify. We explore entity-level partnership terms and allocation trends.

To secure institutional LP commitments, real estate sponsors are increasingly required to provide substantial co-GP equity (typically 5% to 10% of the deal's total equity). For emerging and growing platforms, funding this co-GP requirement can be a massive balance sheet constraint, highlighting the importance of building a diversified investment portfolio.

Unlocking Growth via Entity-Level Capital

Strategic entity-level investments solve this bottleneck. By selling a minority equity stake in the parent platform to a long-term capital provider, the sponsor secures permanent working capital. This balance sheet expansion enables the operator to execute transactions confidently, diversify their holdings, and capture higher GP promotes.

Structuring Key-Person & Governance Terms

For investors, these transactions are structured to capture a blend of GP fee-income, promote overrides, and long-term asset appreciation. Essential terms to negotiate include transfer restrictions, key-person clauses, and exit strategies (like put options or recapitalizations). Done right, entity-level capitalization establishes a launchpad for operator growth and portfolio diversification.
